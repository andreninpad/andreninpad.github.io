Dr. José Rizal

A polyglot and polymath man who liberated the Philippines

Here's a time line of Dr. José Rizal:

  • 1861 – José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born on the 19th day of June 1861, to Francisco Rizal Mercado and Teodora Alonso Realonda y Quintos, in the town of Calamba in Laguna Province.
  • 1864 – He learned the alphabet from his mother, Teodora Alonso Realonda.
  • 1865 – His sister Concepcion died at the age of three. He shed real tears for the first time.
  • 1867 – His mother taught him how to read and write. As he grew older, his parents employed private tutors to give him lessons at home. The first was Maestro Celestino & the second, was Maestro Lucas Padua. Leon Monroy, father's classmate, taught Rizal rudiments of Latin. Uncle Manuel Alberto, concerned with his physical development; taught love for the open air & developed in him a great admiration for nature's beauty, while Uncle Gregorio, a scholar, instilled into his mind a passion and love for education.
  • 1868 – He made a pilgrimage to Antipolo with his father Francisco Mercado Rizal to fulfill the vow made by his mother to take the child to the Shrine of the Virgin of Antipolo should she and her child survive the ordeal of delivery which nearly caused his mother's life.
  • 1869 – At age eight, He wrote his first poem entitled "Sa Aking Mga Kabata." The poem was written in Tagalog and had its theme "Love of One's Language."
  • 1870 – The school was in the teacher's house, a small nipa hut about 30 meters from his aunt's home. His teacher in Biñan was a severe disciplinarian. His name was Justiniano Aquino Cruz. Then, He and his brother went to Manila to enroll and take the entrance exam at San Juan de Letran, but when he went back to Calamba he found out that his father's wish had changed, instead of San Juan de Letran, he wished to enroll him at Ateneo de Manila.
  • 1877 – He studied Philosophy and Letters at the University of Santo Tomas. He graduated at age 16 and received a Bachelor of Arts degree, with highest honors but not a valedictorian.
  • 1878 – He studied the vocational course which led to the title of Porito Agrimensor (Expert Surveyor). He also studied Cosmology, Metaphysics, Theodicy, and History of Philosophy.
  • 1879 – He took up medicine and enrolled simultaneously in a preparatory for the medical and the regular first-year medical course.
  • 1880 – At age 19 he won the first prize in a literary contest in Manila with his masterpiece (essay): El Conseho delos Dioses (The Council of Gods).
  • 1882 – His first travel to Europe joined Circulo Hispano-Filipino and wrote “Mi Piden Versos” (They ask me for Verses) as requested by the members of the society. He traveled to Spain to improve his knowledge of the language, and the arts. He departed for Singapore, on board the Spanish steamer, Salvadora. He enrolled in Medicine at the Universidad Central de Madrid. Then went to Paris where he had a chance to contact the Masonry.
  • 1884 – He completed his medical course in Spain. Universidad Central de Madrid awarded him the degree of Licentiate in Medicine.
  • 1885 – He studied and passed all subjects leading to the degree of Doctor of Medicine. He did not present the thesis required for graduation nor paid the fees, so he was not awarded his Doctor's diploma
  • 1886 – Went To Paris to study Ophthalmology. Wrote "A Las Flores de Heidelberg". Wrote his letter in German to Professor Ferdinand Blumentritt, Director of the Ateneo of Leitmetriz, Australia.
  • 1887 – He traveled alone to study medicine at the Universidad Central de Madrid. There he earned the degree, of Licentiate in Medicine. He also attended medical lectures at the University of Paris and the Heidelberg University. In Berlin, he was inducted as a member of the Berlin Ethnological Society and the Berlin Anthropological Society under the patronage of pathologist Rudolf Virchow. His novel Noli Me Tangere was finished. Then, he began writing his second novel while practicing medicine in Calamba.
  • 1888 – He made some changes to the plot and corrected some chapters in his second novel.
  • 1889 – His pamphlet The Vision of Fr. Rodriguez written under the pen name “Dimasalang” was published in Barcelona to answer the friars' attack on Noli Me Tangere.
  • 1890 – His essay, “The Philippines, a Century Hence” was published in La Solidaridad in four series. He became a Master of Masons in Lodge Solidaridad. His second novel came off the press. He donated the original manuscript and autographed printed copy to Valentin Ventura, the savior of El Filibusterismo.
  • 1891 – He wrote more chapters in Paris and Madrid and finally finished the manuscript. Then he left Brussels for Ghent, a famous university in Belgium.
  • 1892 – He became a Master Mason of Le Grand Orient France in Paris. He organized the civic organization, LA LIGA FILIPINA at the house of Dorotheo Ongjunco in which one of the attendees was Andres Bonifacio, the founding father of the Katipunan.
  • 1893 – His exile in Dapitan, Zamboanga was one of the most lonesome and sorrowful periods of his life. He missed the company of his friends and family, and the death of Leonor Rivera.
  • 1896 – He was tried before the military court under the supervision of Judge Advocate General Nicholas dela Peňa on the grounds of treason, sedition, and the formation of illegal association. He was officially notified of his death sentence rendered by the Spanish military court. Then was executed the next day by firing squad.
"José Rizal is an example of a man who suffered greatly, who fought for freedom and died for it, and who, at the same time, tried to bring about reforms for his people."

-- Mahatma Gandhi
